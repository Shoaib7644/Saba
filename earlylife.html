My Early Life

I was born in Raipur, but before I even turned one, my family moved to Musannah, Oman. Growing up in a new country at such a young age was a unique experience. Oman became my home, a place where I spent my early years exploring, learning, and adapting to a different culture. Despite living in Oman, India was always close to my heart. Every couple of years, I would visit India to meet my relatives, grandmother, and cousins. These trips were always special, filled with love, warmth, and endless memories. I cherished the time spent with family, enjoying Indian traditions, festivals, and all the delicious homemade food that reminded me of my roots. Living in two different countries gave me a unique perspective on life. It taught me adaptability, the importance of family, and a deep appreciation for both Omani and Indian cultures. My early years laid the foundation for the person I am today—someone who embraces diversity and values relationships deeply.
